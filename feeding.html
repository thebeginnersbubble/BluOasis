Fish Feeding Essentials

Explore the Best Types of Food for Every Species! From flakes to pellets and live options, discover how to provide the perfect diet tailored to your fish’s unique needs for optimal health and vitality.

  • Dry Foods
  • Frozen Foods
  • Freeze Dried Foods
  • Live Foods
  • Greens

Before you dive in...
Start by Understanding Their Dietary Needs! Find out whether your fish are meat-eaters (carnivores), vegetation-eaters (herbivores) or both (omnivores) to provide the right diet that supports their health and well-being.

1. Dry Foods

Carnivores, herbivores and omnivores can obtain their nutrition from flakes and pellets (sinking / floating).

Side Note:

Dry fish food can be lower in fibre so it is recommended to add vegetables to your fish’s diet. This will reduce the risk of swim bladder disorders and bloating especially for vegetarian species. Sheets of dried spirulina and nori algae are great options !

2. Frozen Foods

Carnivorous fish would enjoy frozen foods such as frozen shrimp, bloodworms, plankton, prawn, krill and mussels.

Herbivorous fish would best be fed frozen spirulina cubes that are easily found in pet stores.

Side Note:

Frozen foods have a positive effect on coloration. It increases fry growth and is beneficial for your fish friend's immune system!

3. Freeze Dried Foods

Tubifex worms and Mysis shrimp or other foods can be found as freeze dried cubes.

Side Note:

These are typically very nutritious and recommended for carnivorous fish.

4. Live Foods

Live foods options include live brine or ghost shrimp that are especially useful for feeding young or small freshwater fish, crickets and worms which are high in protein and fat. These are perfect for carnivorous fish!

Side Note:

Live foods provide a balanced diet, closely mimicking what fish consume in their natural habitats, nourishing their health and enhancing their colour and vitality!

5. Greens

Greens for fish are inclusive of lettuce, cucumber, zucchini and spinach. If your fish friend munches on aquarium plants (e.g. anacharis), give them greens as well!

Side Note:

Clip greens to the side of the aquarium tank or fasten them in place near the substrate and remember to remove uneaten vegetables within 24 hours of nibbling.
