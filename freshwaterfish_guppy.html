Guppy

Basic Profile
Family Poeciliidae
Origin South America
Physical Characteristics Guppies come in a wide range of colors and combinations, with their fins varying as well. Some have short, rounded fins, while others have long, flowing ones. Male guppies are known for their vibrant colors, longer caudal and dorsal fins, and marbled or striped tail fins. Females, on the other hand, tend to have larger bodies but more subdued coloration.
Full-grown Size 2 to 2.5 inches
Life Span 2 to 5 years
Diet Omnivore
Tank Level Top and middle dwellers
Compatibility
Aggressive level Tank Mates
Peaceful Neon Tetra
Fish Demands
Water Temperature 68 F to 78 F (20 C to 26 C)
Water Hardness 8 dGH to 12 dGH
pH Preferences pH 7.5 to pH 8.0
Minimum Tank Size 2 gallons
Feeding Guppies thrive on high-quality flakes or pellets, along with high-protein snacks such as bloodworms, brine shrimp, and mosquito larvae.

Estimated Price (RM): 1-3.50 per Guppy

Tip:

It's best to keep guppies in groups of at least three for a happier, healthier tank.
